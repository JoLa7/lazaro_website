No. 001

Figuring it out

Stanford, 2025

Identity Reflection
I used to think, when I was older I'd have it all figured out That time would bring clarity & growing up meant growing certain
I used to think, when I was older I'd have it all figured out That time would bring clarity & growing up meant growing certain But the seasons have passed, My friends and family have aged I've moved to a different state Started a new Life, & Learned more than I ever imagined And yet, I haven't figured it out Not even close In fact, the more I know The more I realize how little I understand Each answer leads to more questions Every door opens to more unknowns Sometimes I wonder If I ever will have it all figured out? Maybe not Maybe, thats not the point Maybe, that is what it means to grow older. To not know everything But to make peace with not knowing And to keep going anyways

No. 002

Not Conventional

Stanford, 2026

identity reflection
There is a code, I can't seem to break A way of formulating answers Of making the right observations At the right time
There is a code, I can't seem to break A way of formulating answers Of making the right observations At the right time I once told a friend "I really like your parents house" To which they said: "You are not from here are you?" The acceptable answer, apparently, Was something about Victorian Decorations Steep gables, stained glasses, and ornate trims The vocabulary of belonging There is a notion of prestige I was never handed A rulset written in invisble ink Read fluently by people Who never had to learn it It doesn't matter who I am What matters is if I said house when I should have said home Or something more refined than both I don't fit this mold And I never found the entrance Why am I done with the bay? Maybe because I am tired Of failing a test I did not know I was taking

No. 003

Celebrations

Magnolia, 2023

american dream reflection identity
Growing up, celebrations weren’t Elaborate dinners with silver platters and wine They were in a booth at Burger King
Growing up, celebrations weren’t Elaborate dinners with silver platters and wine They were in a booth at Burger King I never understood why my parents loved it so much To me, it was just a burger But they had grown up in rural Mexico, Where Burger King commercials played With their Whoppers and crowns. Things they could see, But never touch To them, that booth wasn’t a cheap meal It was proof Of something they once only watched through a screen To me, it's just a burger. To them, it's everything They were never supposed to have
